How To Upcycle

Before throwing something out, consider how it can be repurposed. Start small, perhaps by turning empty tins into plant pots or personalizing an old piece of clothing.

Use things you already have, go to thrift shops or antique stores. Shop second-hand; check online sales apps such as Facebook Marketplace, Depop, ThreadUp, Vinted, Poshmark, etc.

There are very few limits to what you can create; some things that are typically upcycled are clothes, furniture, and random miscellaneous items. Depending on what you have, you can create decor, clothing and accessories, storage and organizing units and way more!

A Brief Step-by-Step

1.  Find an item you want to upcycle & think of different things to
        turn it into.
2.  Create a plan; use tutorials, find pattern pieces, make sure you
          have all the equipment and materials you need to create your
          project. Remember: measure twice, cut once!
3.   Clean and prepare your item; clean it, iron it, cut off loose/ spare
           parts, etc.
4a.   If you’re painting an item, start by sanding it to create a rough
               surface for the paint to stick to.
4b.   If you’re sewing something, find or make pattern pieces to create
               a cutting template. Cut out your pieces and start sewing (make
               sure you have enough fabric before starting).
5.   Start cutting, sanding, painting, sewing, gluing, and combining all
           of your elements together to create something new.
6.   Seal, hem, and finish your upcycled project.

Clothes

Some ways to upcycle clothes include:
• Turning old pants or a shirt into a reusable grocery/tote bag
• Mending a hole or covering a stain with patchwork or Sashiko
• Thrift flips: adjusting the cut or fit of an article of clothing to
    make it fit you and your personal style better
• Shortening pants and cropping shirts
• Turning jeans into a corset top or a sweater into a vest
• Repurposing an old scarf into a camera or camcorder strap
• Turning scrap fabric into patchwork material which can
   be made into anything you want (as long as you have enough fabric)

Furniture

Some ways to upcycle furniture include:
• Refurbish old furniture
• Repair cracks, chips, broken pieces & parts, etc
• Paint or redecorate a piece of furniture to fit a new style
• Old tires can be transformed into stylish and durable furniture
   pieces, such as ottomans, chairs, and coffee tables.
• Repurpose old drawers into basement or under-bed storage
• Turn old drawers, handles, or shelving materials into
   a DIY coat rack
• Turn an old crib into toy, craft, or clothing storage

Miscellaneous Items

Some ways to upcycle your miscellaneous items include:
• Turn empty food cans into storage/organizers
• Turn watering can into a flower pot
• Empty glass bottles can be upcycled into planters
• Old musical instruments, like guitars or pianos, can be turned into
   shelves, tables, or other functional decor pieces.
• Wood Pallets, often discarded after use, can be upcycled into
    various home decor items like rustic picture frames, wall art, and
    even garden furniture.
• Hang old teacups, coffee cups, or tin cans from hooks and twine
    as patio bird feeders
• Turn old wine corks into new drink coasters
